<p><p>Solid Oxide Fuel Cells (SOFC) are devices that produce electricity from a chemical reaction between fuel, typically hydrogen or natural gas, and oxygen. SOFCs can be classified into three types based on their design and structure. Tubular SOFCs consist of a tubular-shaped electrolyte, while planar SOFCs have flat, stackable cells. Other types include coaxial and planar-vertical SOFCs. These different designs offer varying advantages, including higher power density, improved heat transfer, and lower manufacturing costs. The choice of SOFC type depends on specific application requirements and desired performance characteristics.</p></p>
