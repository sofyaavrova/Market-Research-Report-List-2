<p><p>Rice oil is extracted from rice bran, a byproduct of rice milling. There are two main types of rice oil market methods: extraction and squeezing. Extraction involves using solvents like hexane to separate the oil from the bran, resulting in a higher quantity of oil but with potentially lower quality. Squeezing, on the other hand, involves using mechanical force to squeeze the oil out of the bran, resulting in a smaller yield but a more natural and higher quality oil. The choice between the two methods depends on factors such as cost, efficiency, and desired oil quality.</p></p>
