<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The power-assisted bicycle market consists of two main types: mid-drive and hub-drive. Mid-drive refers to a system where the motor is located in the middle of the bike's frame, typically near the pedals. This design offers better weight distribution, improved handling, and efficient power transfer. On the other hand, hub-drive involves a motor located in either the front or rear wheel hub. This design provides simplicity, ease of maintenance, and a more natural riding experience. Both types have their unique advantages, catering to different preferences and needs of riders.</p></p>
