<p><p>Linear Voltage Controlled Oscillator (VCO) is a device used to generate an oscillating electrical signal whose frequency can be controlled by an applied voltage. There are three main types of VCOs in the market:</p><p>1. LC-tank Oscillators: These VCOs utilize an inductor-capacitor (LC) tank circuit to generate the oscillations. They are simple, cost-effective, and widely used in various applications like radio transmitters and receivers.</p><p>2. Crystal Oscillators: These VCOs use a quartz crystal resonator to maintain a stable frequency output. They are highly accurate and exhibit low phase noise, making them suitable for applications that require precise timing, such as communication systems and digital devices.</p><p>3. Other Market: This category includes VCOs based on different technologies like MEMS (Micro-Electro-Mechanical Systems) and SAW (Surface Acoustic Wave). MEMS-based VCOs offer small size and lower power consumption, making them suitable for portable devices. SAW-based VCOs provide excellent frequency stability and are commonly used in wireless communication systems.</p></p>
