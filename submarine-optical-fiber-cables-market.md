<p><p>The Submarine Optical Fiber Cables market can be classified into two types: Deep-Sea Fiber Optic Cable and Shallow Sea Optic Cable. Deep-Sea Fiber Optic Cables are specifically designed to be laid at greater depths of the ocean, typically beyond 200 meters. They are more robust and capable of withstanding harsh conditions. Shallow Sea Optic Cables, on the other hand, are deployed in relatively shallower waters, within 200 meters deep. They are less complex and less expensive compared to deep-sea cables.</p></p>
