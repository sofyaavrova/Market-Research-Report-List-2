<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>The CMOS image sensor market consists of two main types: front-illuminated (FI) CMOS sensors and backside-illuminated (BSI) CMOS sensors. FI CMOS sensors have a traditional structure, where the wiring layers are placed above the photodiode, limiting light absorption. On the other hand, BSI CMOS sensors have a flipped design, where the wiring layers are positioned behind the photodiode, enhancing light sensitivity. BSI CMOS sensors are generally more efficient in low-light conditions and offer better image quality. Both types cater to various industries, including smartphones, digital cameras, and medical imaging.</p></p>
