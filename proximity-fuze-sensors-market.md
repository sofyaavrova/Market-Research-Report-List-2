<p><ul><li>Pulsed Doppler</li><li>Continuous Wave Fuze</li><li>Frequency Modulated Continuous Wave</li><li>Pulsed Laser Fuze</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Proximity fuze sensors are used for accurately detonating explosives by sensing the proximity of targets. The market types include Pulsed Doppler, which senses targets based on the frequency change in returned signals; Continuous Wave Fuze, which emits and receives signals simultaneously to measure the target's distance; Frequency Modulated Continuous Wave, which transmits signals across a range of frequencies for improved accuracy; Pulsed Laser Fuze, which uses laser pulses to determine target distance; and Others, which encompass alternative proximity sensing technologies such as infrared or acoustic sensors. These sensors enhance military operations by ensuring precision and minimizing collateral damage.</p></p>
