<p><p>Overhead conductors are wires or cables that are used to transmit electrical power over long distances. These conductors are primarily used in power distribution and transmission systems, where they are installed on transmission towers or poles and carry electricity from power plants to substations, industries, and households. Overhead conductors are made of materials that possess high electrical conductivity, tensile strength, and corrosion resistance, such as aluminum, steel, and composite materials.</p><p>The overhead conductors market has witnessed significant growth in recent years due to the increasing demand for electricity, rapid urbanization, and industrialization. The growing need for reliable and efficient power transmission and distribution systems is also driving market growth. Moreover, the development of renewable energy sources, such as wind and solar power, has further augmented the demand for overhead conductors.</p><p>Key trends in the overhead conductors market include the increasing adoption of high-temperature conductors, such as aluminum conductor composite core (ACCC) and high-temperature low-sag (HTLS) conductors, which offer enhanced efficiency and power carrying capacity. The integration of advanced technologies, such as fiber optics, in overhead conductors to facilitate real-time monitoring and fault detection is another emerging trend in the market.</p><p>The overhead conductors market is expected to exhibit a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 4.5% during the forecast period. The market growth is attributed to various factors, such as infrastructure development, investments in renewable energy projects, and government initiatives to electrify rural areas. Additionally, the increasing focus on grid modernization and upgrading aging transmission infrastructure is expected to drive the demand for overhead conductors in the coming years.</p></p>
