For instance, the development of PERC (Passivated Emitter Rear Cell) technology has significantly increased the efficiency of solar modules, making them more cost-effective.</p><p>In addition, the falling prices of solar modules have made them more affordable, further propelling market growth. The declining cost of solar panels is mainly attributed to economies of scale, improved manufacturing processes, and technological advancements.</p><p>Furthermore, the market is witnessing a shift towards the use of bifacial solar modules, which can generate electricity from both sides of the panel. These modules have the potential to deliver higher energy yields and offer more design flexibility.</p><p>Overall, the solar module market is expected to experience steady growth in the coming years, driven by increasing renewable energy adoption, technological advancements, and declining prices. Polycrystalline Silicon modules are made from multiple crystal structures, offering a lower price point but slightly lower efficiency.
