<p><strong>The GPIO Expanders Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Quasi-bidirectional GPIO</li><li>LED PWM Open-drain GPIO</li></ul></p>
<p><p>GPIO expanders are devices that allow for more GPIO pins to be added to a microcontroller or a system-on-a-chip. Quasi-bidirectional GPIO refers to expanders that can be configured as inputs or outputs individually, whereas LED PWM expanders are specifically designed for controlling LED brightness using pulse-width modulation. Open-drain GPIO expanders are capable of signaling low or becoming high-impedance, which is useful for applications like I2C communication. These different types of GPIO expanders cater to specific needs and provide flexibility in terms of input/output configurations and LED control.</p></p>
