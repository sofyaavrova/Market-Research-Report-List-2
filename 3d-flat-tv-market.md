<p><p>The 3D flat TV market can be broadly categorized into two types: glasses-based and naked eye-based. Glasses-based 3D TVs require viewers to wear special glasses that synchronize with the TV screen to create a 3D effect. These glasses can be either active (powered by batteries) or passive (polarized lenses). On the other hand, naked eye-based 3D TVs use advanced technology to create the illusion of depth without the need for glasses. These TVs utilize different techniques such as lenticular lenses or autostereoscopic displays to deliver a 3D viewing experience directly to the naked eye.</p></p>
