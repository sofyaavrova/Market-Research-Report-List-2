<p><p>A photoelectric coupler, also known as an optocoupler, is an electronic component that transfers electrical signals between two isolated circuits using light. It consists of a light emitter and a light detector, separated by a transparent medium. When an electrical signal is applied to the input side, the light emitter emits photons that are detected by the light detector, which then converts them back into an electrical signal on the output side. This isolation between input and output circuits provides protection against electrical noise and voltage spikes.</p><p>The current outlook of the photoelectric coupler market is favorable, with increasing adoption of optocouplers in various industries such as telecommunications, automotive, and consumer electronics. The market for photoelectric couplers can be categorized into two types: External Optical Path Photoelectric Coupler and Internal Optical Path Photoelectric Coupler. </p><p>External Optical Path Photoelectric Couplers have the light emitter and the photodetector physically separated by an external transparent medium, while the Internal Optical Path Photoelectric Couplers have the emitter and detector placed within the same package.
